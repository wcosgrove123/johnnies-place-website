Mission Statement

Creating safe, supportive housing and meaningful community connections

Johnnie's Place will provide first class, safe, residential housing at reasonable market rates and costs for 1-2 adults diagnosed with and on the Autism Spectrum, who have secured the consolidated Medicaid waiver for support services, staffing needs and caretaking.

In addition, each house established by the organization will identify and serve 2-5 additional "house" members and will provide social, recreational and vocational activities both within the dwelling, and in the local community through connection with local businesses, community organizations, service providers, and volunteers.

The residents will be charged market rent, and the house members and residents will be charged an annual membership / activity fee as stipulated by the organization.

In addition, the organization will provide support and training for the family and caretakers of the residents and the house members.

What We Provide

Comprehensive Support Services

Safe Housing

First class, safe residential housing at reasonable market rates for adults on the Autism Spectrum who have secured the consolidated Medicaid waiver for support services.

Staffing & Caretaking

Professional support services, staffing needs and caretaking to ensure the safety, wellbeing, and development of all residents.

Social Activities

Social, recreational and vocational activities both within the dwelling and in the local community.

Community Connection

Connection with local businesses, community organizations, service providers, and volunteers to build meaningful relationships.

Family Training

Support and training for the family and caretakers of the residents and house members.

House Membership

Each house will serve 2-5 additional "house" members beyond the primary residents, creating a true community experience.

Looking Ahead

Future Expansion

Annual fundraising will support the recreational, social and vocational programming. In time, a part-time director / coordinator will be hired to oversee the activity programming and partnership with the community.

Expansion of future residences is a distinct possibility. The model we're creating at Johnnie's Place is designed to be replicated, providing a blueprint for other communities to follow.

Current Focus

Establishing our first residence at 318 East Elm Street, Conshohocken

Long-term Vision

Creating a replicable model for expansion to serve more adults with autism
